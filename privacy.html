Privacy Policy

Last updated: May 30, 2026

MinuteGlass is designed as a local-first timer app.

Data Collection

MinuteGlass does not collect personal data.

The app does not use accounts, analytics SDKs, advertising SDKs, tracking technologies, third-party login, remote quote APIs, or external databases.

Data Stored On Device

MinuteGlass stores timer preferences on your device so the app can remember your settings. This may include selected timer duration, selected color theme, selected dial shape, selected alarm sound, notification preference, vibration and haptic preferences, and appearance preference.

This information is stored locally on your device and is not sent to the developer.

Notifications

MinuteGlass can use local notifications to alert you when a timer finishes.

Notifications are optional. The app asks for notification permission only when you enable notifications. Notification settings are stored on your device and are not sent to the developer.

Network Access

MinuteGlass does not contact external servers for its core timer experience.

Short messages shown in the app are bundled with the app and do not require network access.

Children

MinuteGlass does not knowingly collect data from children. Because the app does not collect personal data, no personal data from children is collected by the developer.

Changes

This policy may be updated if MinuteGlass changes its data practices. If analytics, crash reporting, advertising, accounts, cloud sync, or external APIs are added in the future, this policy and the App Store privacy information must be updated before release.
